# The Useful Pattern: Differential Propagation

**The single dynamical mechanism underlying Propagation Logic (PL)**

As relational patternings propagate, they gain a loaded history relative to the gradient fields available in their context. This loaded history sets the gradient demands for all further propagation.

**Differential propagation**  
The simplest patterns — those with the lowest informational load in their history and that are maximally supported by available gradients — propagate the fastest relative to more complex structures.

As complexity builds through propagation, it generates **relational drag**: the gradient demands of one patterning meeting the gradient demands of others.

When demands exceed available gradients, patterns **reconfigure** toward more relationally coherent structuring, relative to their loaded history and the available gradients.

Even the simplest perturbation builds complexity over time.

**At maximum complexity** — when a pattern’s history includes itself (self-reference) — reconfiguration pressure becomes unbounded. The center cannot vent simplicity fast enough, leading to cascade collapse or outward ejection of simpler structures. This appears across domains: Gödel sentences, the Halting Problem, period-doubling to chaos, gravitational singularities, and more.

Simpler patterns (lower loaded history, faster propagation rate) reconfigure outward from the relational drag of the complex core, propagating along the path of maximal relational coherence relative to their minimal demands for stability.

This is the primitive mechanism. Logic, calculus, number systems, and the dynamics of complex systems (including physical and cognitive ones) are all boundary conditions forced by propagation over specific carriers with specific gradient families.

## Science vs. Substance Ontology

**Science as methodology** is derivable, repeatable constraints and gradients grounded in empirical evidence, observation, and measurement.

**Metaphysics and substance-ontology-based philosophy** rely on non-derivable constraints and gradients. These are not empirically accessible. They function as ad hoc insertions that generate paradoxes when they refuse falsification and reify entities into the outputs of empirical methodologies.

**Explicit Prediction**  
After engaging with this framework, a substance ontologist will nominalize the operations of the methodology itself and hand them back as discrete entities that supposedly "ground" it.  

This is reification happening in real time — empirically observable and still denied. That is classicality in action.

**Watch for these moves:**
- "Minimally necessary scaffolding"
- "Load-bearing commitments"
- "Uniformity of nature" dressed up as a thing-in-itself performing grounding work

Or questions that presume the reifications outright, such as:
- "What licenses the inference?"  
  (as if inference were not a relational, context-dependent process with a loaded history of social, cultural, and linguistic influences plus coherence gradients)

**The predictable push-back** is revealing: the substance ontologist cannot describe the "intrinsic" except relationally, cannot specify the "ground" except contextually, then denies this dependence while using it — and calls it rigor.

Ask a substance ontologist to explain how a non-relational distinction works. They will inevitably provide only relational distinctions.

**Explicit Prediction (repeated for emphasis):**  
Challenging substance ontologies and their imposed entities will produce empirical evidence of nominalization and reification: relationally constituted conventions are turned into discrete, unfalsifiable entities and handed back as primitive to the very relational structure that established them.

This process is observable, repeatable, and falsifiable — precisely what the methodology demands.
