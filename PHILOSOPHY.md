# Philosophy of Propagation Logic

**Process First — Substance Never**

Propagation Logic is not another formal system added to the existing toolkit of mathematics and logic. It is an explicit rejection of the substance ontology that has dominated Western thought since Aristotle and a return to **process as the fundamental reality**.

### Core Ontological Commitment

In PL, there are no static objects, no eternal propositions, no completed sets, and no timeless numbers that “exist” independently.  
The only primitives are **propagating patterns** engaged in continuous **becoming**.

A pattern `P = (v_P, L_P)` is not a thing that *has* a value and an extra piece of data called “load.”  
The **informational load** `L_P` **is** the accumulated history and intensity of the propagation process itself. It is the living trace of what the pattern has undergone. The designation component `v_P` is only a transient stabilization — a momentary “being” that emerges from the flux.

“Being” is therefore secondary and derivative. **Becoming** is primary.

### The Single Mechanistic Driver

All structure arises from one observable process:

- Every propagation step extends the history (load increases).
- When accumulated load exceeds the coherence threshold `θ_C` of the current context, **reconfiguration pressure** appears: `demand(P, C) = max(0, L_P − θ_C)`.
- This pressure is not an added rule — it is the ontological motor of change. The pattern is forced to seek a new, more coherent regime.

There are no axioms imposed from outside. Classical logic laws (non-contradiction, excluded middle, modus ponens), intuitionistic restrictions, modal topologies, probabilistic measures, and the entire apparatus of calculus (differentiation, integration, root-finding) are all **forced stabilizations** that the same mechanistic process produces under different boundary conditions (carrier set `V` and family of gradient fields `G`).

### De-reification

PL systematically de-reifies mathematical objects:
- A “proposition” is not a fixed entity; it is a pattern in propagation.
- A “number” or “function” is not a completed totality; it is a stabilized flow of load under arithmetic gradients.
- Even “proof” is not a static object but a chain of coherence-preserving propagations.
- Gödel incompleteness and the appearance of infinities are both manifestations of maximum complexity — patterns whose history has grown to include themselves.

### Contrast with Substance Ontology

| Substance Ontology (Traditional)      | Process Ontology (Propagation Logic)                  |
|---------------------------------------|-------------------------------------------------------|
| Static objects with properties        | Propagating patterns in continuous becoming           |
| Axioms imposed from outside           | Laws emerge as forced coherence stabilizations        |
| Completed totalities (sets, functions)| Open-ended propagation with accumulating history      |
| Being is primary                      | Becoming is primary; being is transient stabilization |
| Separate foundations for logic & calculus | Single mechanism; logic and calculus are boundary cases |

### Why This Matters

By grounding everything in process and mechanism, PL dissolves many long-standing philosophical puzzles:
- The “unreasonable effectiveness” of mathematics becomes natural — mathematics simply describes the most coherent ways patterns can propagate.
- The unity of logic and calculus is no longer a miracle but a direct consequence of using the same load-combination rules over different carrier sets.
- Questions of foundations shift from “What must we assume?” to “What stable regimes does the propagation process inevitably reach?”

Propagation Logic therefore offers not merely a new formalism, but a new way of seeing reality itself: a world of ongoing, relational, coherence-seeking processes rather than a collection of fixed things.
