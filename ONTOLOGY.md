# Ontology of Propagation Logic

**Process-Relational Ontology**

Fundamental entities: **propagating patterns** engaged in ongoing becoming.

A pattern `P = (v_P, L_P)` consists of:
- `v_P` — transient designation / stabilization component
- `L_P` — informational load = accumulated propagation history (the process itself)

No substances, no atemporal objects. All mathematical structure is generated by the primitive mechanistic operator `P / G → Q` operating within a context `C = (Γ_C, o_C, θ_C)`.

**Key ontological primitives**
- Propagation extends history (load accumulation).
- Coherence threshold `θ_C` defines regimes of stability.
- Reconfiguration pressure `demand = max(0, L_P − θ_C)` is the intrinsic driver of ontological change.
- Gradient fields `G` encode possible ways of becoming.

Logic and calculus are not distinct domains but different coherent boundary regimes of the **same underlying process dynamics**.

This ontology is explicitly **de-reifying**: mathematical “objects” are reduced to stabilized patterns of propagation. Laws are not axioms but necessary consequences of load combination under coherence pressure.
